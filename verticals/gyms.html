Gyms & studios

People breathe 10 times harder when working out. In a sealed gym, CO₂ spikes fast and particles stay airborne. Ventilation isn't optional — it's critical.

10x breathing rate Fast CO₂ spikes Airborne sweat particles

The problem

Why your members feel tired faster

Sealed cardio rooms and yoga studios accumulate CO₂ within 20 minutes of class start.

3,500+

CO₂ in cardio rooms

20 people on cardio machines push CO₂ past 3,500 ppm in 15 minutes. Members tire faster, leave earlier.

10x

Faster breathing

Exercisers inhale 10x more air than resting. That means 10x more pollutants absorbed in the same time.

Sweat

Airborne transmission

Droplets from heavy breathing stay airborne longer in humid gym environments. No ventilation = no clearance.

Our solution

High-exchange ventilation for high-effort spaces

Faster air changes. Humidity control. Airborne particle clearance. All without cold drafts.

01

8-12 air changes per hour

Much higher than residential. Keeps CO₂ below 900 ppm even during peak classes.

02

Humidity management

Reclaims moisture from exhaust air. No dry air in winter, no swampy feel in summer.

03

UV-C in high-traffic zones

Optional sterilization reduces airborne pathogen load. Relevant post-pandemic.

04

Zone-based for studios vs weights

Yoga studios run quieter. Weights rooms get higher flow. Each zone tuned to its activity.
